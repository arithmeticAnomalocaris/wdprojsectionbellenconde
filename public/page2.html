Suzanna Spencer

Suzanna Spencer is one of Queen Victoria's women-of-the-bedchamber and the fiancée of one of Her private secretarial officers.

Appearance:

Suzanna is a young woman with a slender build and pale complexion. She has wine-red eyes, long eyelashes, and straight, dark brown hair, which she ties up in a neat bun, save for loose strands on the side of her face. She has straight, square bangs, which are parted slightly in the middle and cover her eyebrows. Suzanna dresses in a white, low-cut evening gown with short sleeves and long, white gloves, and a bustle train at the back. On her head, she wears 3 feathers, with a white veil attached that goes below her waistline.

Personality:

Suzanna is a calm woman, with a friendly demeanor and gentle smile always appearing on her delicate countenance, genteel expected of a noblewoman such as herself. She is staunchly loyal to Queen Victoria, due to her mother’s influence, and dutifully carries out tasks assigned to her with aptitude and acuity. However, her pragmatism often manifests to a fault, with her methods possessing a concerning apathy for the emotional and physical wellbeing of those involved, as well as general moral standards. She has no qualms against lying, cheating, seducing, or killing if it accomplishes her goals, and often suggests others take courses of action that similarly have no concern for moral conventions. She is shown to be serene regardless of the situation, maintaining her general apathy even in the face of the brutality she often commits towards enemies of the Queen. Despite her nigh sociopathy, Suzanna is shown to be somewhat religious, referring to the teachings of the Bible, especially the Ten Commandments, to guide her actions on the surface, however failing to follow through with their meanings when the situation calls for it. Furthermore, despite her lack of self-awareness, Suzanna is incredibly perceptive when it comes to others, able to gauge what sort of responses and actions would elicit the outcome she desires from the people around her. She refrains from fighting unless negotiation proves inadequate, and dislikes losing competitions, similar to her fiancé, however she expresses her frustration in more subtle methods.

Abilities:

Suzanna, despite her slender frame, is shown to be a skilled and agile combatant with knowledge of a wide range of weapons, and often utilizes a mace in-battle. In case of a lack of weapons, she is shown to be capable of using her surroundings to her advantage.

History (Backstory)

Suzanna was born to Baroness Churchill and her husband, a few years prior to her brother. Throughout her early youth, Suzanna would often engage in “troublesome behaviors”, and show a general lack of remorse for her actions. Baroness Churchill intervened after an incident involving her operating on some small animals to observe their insides, instilling a deep sense of religion within her to act as a guideline for her moral compass. This sense of religion, while waning in its intensity, still is followed by Suzanna towards her adult years. Suzanna during her youth would also take several courses deemed worthy by her mother, all in preparation for her future career as the latter’s successor as Lady-of-the-bedchamber in the Queen’s court. Thus, amidst her sewing, painting, and general feminine education, she was made to assume the disposition of a male, in order to prevent her reputation’s decline, and learn how to fence, shoot, and other skills typically taught to noblemen. At some point, she was betrothed to her fiancé.

Relationships:

  • Lady Churchill (Mother) - Suzanna has a surprisingly positive relationship with her mother, with the latter still caring deeply about her despite Suzanna’s eccentricities, which she learned to use to her and Suzanna’s advantage. She is one of the reasons for Suzanna’s moral apathy, encouraging such action in order to ensure the prosperity of the “bigger picture”.

  • Fiancé- Despite the differences in their respective dispositions, Suzanna and her fiancé are alike in many facets and are shown to care about each other deeply, despite the flawed ways in which they express their affections. He is shown to enjoy her presence, and she him, and they allow each other both a challenge and a respite to each other. Overall, both share a pride in each other’s accomplishments, and are able to find a dynamic that works for the both of them.

Trivia:

  • ”Suzanna” is derived from the Egyptian shoshen, meaning “Iris Flower”.
  • The surname “Spencer” refers to someone who dispensed the provisions in affluent households, such as house stewards or butlers.
  • Suzanna may be descended from Lady Jane Spencer, Baroness Churchill, who was the longest-serving Lady-of-the-Bedchamber of Queen Victoria.
  • She is naturally left-handed, however she was taught to use her right, making her ambidextrous.
  • She dislikes sweets, preferring bitter food. Her favorite drink is black coffee.
  • She is a skilled harpist, having started at the age of 9.
  • She had a passing interest in human psychology, specifically works by Adolphe Garnier, in her youth. Such interest was what drove her to attempt to operate on a small animal’s brain.
