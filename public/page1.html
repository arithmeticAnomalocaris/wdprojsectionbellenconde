Tsuzuru Hokorobu

Tsuzuru Hokorobu (綻ぶ つづる, Hokorobu Tsuzuru) is a miko from Hanomiya Shrine in the Taisho Era.

Appearance:

Tsuzuru is a short girl of average height with a pale complexion and light blue eyes. She wears her medium-length dark brown hair in a loose ponytail, with her bangs swept to the sides of her face. She is usually seen wearing a white chihaya atop traditional miko attire, however when on leave, she switches this out for a light purple kimono with numerous flower patterns, underneath a dark magenta hifu.

Personality:

Tsuzuru, when on duty, presents herself as an enigmatic person prone to odd, but insightful equivoques that ultimately end up assisting the people around her despite their initial perplexions. While she often does things for her own amusement, she holds herself back in favor of dedicating herself to her duties, and refers to others very formally regardless of their age or status. She is also shown to dislike it when others look down on her, and is not afraid to coldly reprimand others when they speak or act poorly towards her or other people.

However, she later reveals herself to be a more carefree individual, concerned about the amount of responsibility she is meant to accept in her near future, and hopes to make the most of her childhood while she still is able to. She is envious of her predecessor Amane, whom she believes readily accepted her duties to the Demon Slayer Corps and was willing to die for the sake of their goals, and feels guilt for being reluctant to do the same. Despite this, she remains cheerful in the face of such difficulty and enjoys partaking in eccentric and somewhat childish activities and befriending others.

Abilities:

Tsuzuru is a skilled marksman, able to shoot using her bow and arrow to distances hundreds of meters away with accuracy and precision.

Trivia:

  • Tsuzuru’s surname contains the Kun’yomi for “unravel, bloom, smile” (綻ぶ, Hokorobu). Her first name contains the hiragana characters for “tsu” (つ), “zu” (づ), and “ru” (る) respectively.
  • Her favorite snack is hoshigaki, a variation of Japanese dried persimmon.
  • Her favorite food is kitsune udon, which is a thick japanese wheat noodle with a mild broth topped with aburaage (sweet dried tofu pouches). She dislikes cold weather and food and wears lots of layers to stay warm.
  • She enjoys listening to Rakugo, especially ghost stories.
  • She dislikes lightning and bad weather because it affects her performance as an archer. She is also afraid of thunder however doesn’t want to admit it.
  • When she is nervous, she doesn’t show it on her face, however she will notably start playing with her own hair (or other peoples’ hair).
